Experience Study

Participants: 148
Duration: 6 weeks
Method: Mixed-methods
Scope: United States

A comparative UX study examining ordering flows, app experiences, in-store environments, and loyalty program friction across four major U.S. coffee chains. Research included usability testing, contextual inquiry, NPS surveys, and heuristic evaluation.

06
Key Pain Points
Top friction areas surfaced through usability sessions and user interviews
Starbucks
⚠
Menu overcrowding: 400+ items causes decision paralysis. New users spend avg. 3.4 min on menu before ordering.
SEVERITY · HIGH · 78% of new users affected
⚠
Order modification cascade: changing one customization resets others unexpectedly, causing frustration and re-entry.
SEVERITY · MEDIUM · 54% of power users affected
⚠
Reward redemption buried under 3 taps; 22% of users didn't know they had rewards available.
SEVERITY · MEDIUM · Loyalty drop-off risk
Dutch Bros
⚠
No mobile pre-order for walk-in locations. Drive-thru queue times exceed 8 min during peak hours with no visibility.
SEVERITY · HIGH · Primary churn driver
⚠
Secret/unofficial menu creates anxiety for new customers who feel pressure to know items that aren't listed.
SEVERITY · MEDIUM · Barrier to new user acquisition
⚠
App inconsistency between iOS and Android; Android users report 40% more friction completing rewards check-in.
SEVERITY · MEDIUM · Platform parity issue
Peet's Coffee
⚠
Points-to-reward conversion opaque; 61% of loyalty members couldn't explain how to redeem without help.
SEVERITY · HIGH · Loyalty value perception gap
⚠
Mobile app checkout flow requires 7 taps vs Starbucks' 4; cart abandonment 2.3× higher than benchmark.
SEVERITY · HIGH · Revenue impact
⚠
Limited social/sharing features disconnects brand's strong community identity from its digital presence.
SEVERITY · LOW · Brand coherence issue
Coffee Bean & Tea Leaf
🔴
App crashes reported by 38% of users during checkout. 19% abandoned permanently after crash loss of order data.
SEVERITY · CRITICAL · Direct revenue loss
🔴
Store hours on app inaccurate for 31% of tested locations; users arrived at closed stores during listed open hours.
SEVERITY · CRITICAL · Trust-breaking experience
⚠
No order history means repeat customers must rebuild orders manually each visit; no personalization engine.
SEVERITY · HIGH · Loyalty & retention risk

08
Strategic Recommendations
Prioritized UX improvements derived from research findings
↑ High Priority
Rebuild CBTL App from Ground Up
The Coffee Bean app requires a full architectural overhaul. Critical bugs, missing features (order history, real-time info), and accessibility failures make incremental fixes insufficient. Recommend a phased rebuild with clear feature parity goals.
Coffee Bean
↑ High Priority
Add Drive-Thru Queue Transparency
Dutch Bros' biggest experience gap is wait time uncertainty. Implementing real-time queue position or estimated wait times — visible before joining the queue — would directly reduce abandonment and drive loyalty among time-sensitive users.
Dutch Bros
↑ High Priority
Simplify Peet's Rewards Redemption
Surface reward availability and one-tap redemption at checkout. Eliminate the requirement to navigate to a separate loyalty screen. Consider a persistent balance indicator on the home screen and push notifications as rewards become redeemable.
Peet's Coffee
→ Medium Priority
Starbucks Menu Curation & Guided Discovery
Introduce a "Quick Pick" or AI-guided recommendation layer for new and infrequent users. Behavioral filtering ("I like sweet and creamy") reduces cognitive load without removing customization depth for power users.
Starbucks
→ Medium Priority
Dutch Bros: Publish the Secret Menu
The barrier of not knowing "secret" drinks actively deters new customers. Officially publishing popular off-menu items in-app with community context (who invented it, when) transforms anxiety into delight and strengthens brand story.
Dutch Bros
→ Medium Priority
Cross-Brand: Accessibility Audit & Remediation
Only Starbucks achieves WCAG AA compliance. Dutch Bros, Peet's, and Coffee Bean should conduct accessibility audits and prioritize screen reader support, contrast ratios, and tap target sizes. Compliance also reduces legal risk exposure significantly.
Dutch BrosPeet'sCoffee Bean
↓ Longer Term
Peet's: Invest in Digital Brand Voice
Peet's best-in-class in-store experience and product quality doesn't translate digitally. Developing a content strategy — origin stories, brewing education, seasonal narratives — would close the gap between physical warmth and app coldness.
Peet's Coffee
↓ Longer Term
Dutch Bros: Expand Ordering Channels
As Dutch Bros expands into urban markets with walk-in formats, the absence of mobile-ahead ordering becomes a competitive liability. A phased rollout of mobile ordering for non-drive-thru locations should be scoped for 2025–2026 expansion markets.
Dutch Bros
↓ Longer Term
Industry: Order Modification UX Standards
The entire category lacks consistent patterns for complex beverage customization. A shared UX pattern library (similar to Material Design but for F&B ordering) would benefit consumers across all apps by reducing re-learning costs when switching between chains.
All Brands
